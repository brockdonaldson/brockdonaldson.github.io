The Kitsune App

[CHALLENGE]

To create a product that allows users to  track their daily commute, while focusing on understanding health and fitness trends that arise through alternative transportation.

Using the app is as simple as connecting to the board and syncing a recent trip.

Through the Kitsune app you are able to track your most used routes, trip efficiency, and fitness. The smartwatch capability allows for ease of use while riding; providing the user with visualized trip information such as estimated arrival time, current trip location, and map view.

[APP FUNCTIONALITY]

While the board acts as a solid product on its own the true value come out of the sycronous use with the companion application. The Kitsune App allows users to gain multiple different improvements to their daily commute. Three overall functions act as the core basis to the Kitsune Board and App.

Travel Logs
An asynchronous connection between board and app will allow users to record travel metrics after each trip. In doing so the app will gather metrics from both the user and board to create an accurate travel history.

Smarter Trips
The more you use the board the quicker it learns and create smarter routes. Based on past trip data the Kitsune App will be able to calculate which route would be best in current travel conditions such as weather and traffic.

Health / Fitness
The app also focuses on health and fitness through calorie and goal based metrics. The Kitsune App also allows users to connect using smartwatch; this capability allows for users to track more indepth health and fitness data such as heart rate.

[COLLABORATION]

In the past couple years there has been a growing trend of sharing and socializing in health and fitness apps. Apps such as Nike+ Run Club and Strava allow users to create goals, track fitness statistics, but with the added bonus of recording and sharing their experience with others.

In a sense, not only do these apps help for individual growth and improvement but they allow users to find many individuals that are challenging themselves in the same way. Looking into a sharing capability not only allows product awareness but also creates a connected network of users experience and growing together.

[INITIAL SKETCHES]

I began the Kitsune app by focusing on 5 key screens: Login Screen, a dashboard displaying most recent ride data, route navigation, an account page, and a ride analytic page. I chose these five screens as my starting point as they act as the entry point for many of the sub level screens.

Through these initial draft screens I was able to get a handle on how to incorporate planned features, as well as, see how features thought of in the planning phase will have to be altered or changed slightly.

The Board and App communitcate through installed hardware in the body of the board.

Mark 01
In the initial stages of the board design I started by taking my designs and importing them into Rhinoceros. While the learning curve for this program was slightly challenging, it was convenient that you could work seamlessly between Adobe Illustrator and Rhino. It allowed me to be able to design paths in Illustrator and work with them in Rhino.

Mark 02
For the hinge to be connected to the bottom of the board, I added two attachments to the underside of the prototype that will hold the joint in place. While the pieces may look large in this rendering, their actual height is only 1.25’’.

Folding allows for ease of portability while at the same time helps create a stronger user & product relationship.
